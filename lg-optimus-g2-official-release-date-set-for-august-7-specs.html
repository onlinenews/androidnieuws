LG Optimus G2 official release date set for August 7 Specs

LG Optimus G2 official release date set for August 7: Specs

LG Optimus G2 official release date is set for August 7. Rumors suggest that the handset will come with top specs and great features

LG’s next generation Android flagship the Optimus G2 is planned for August 7 release. The South Korean tech maker has already sent in press invites for the event. Meanwhile, the much-touted handset would make its way to Korean stores on August 20, two weeks after its global release. More official information about the device is yet to come.

By the way, specs of this Galaxy S4 rival from LG have leaked to web many times. On contrary to the older reports, the phone is likely to tout a bit larger 5.2-inch IPS display with 1080p resolution. Also, in the leaked pics, the device is seen as featuring very thin bezels and no physical keys. If it is button-less, LG would really be taking an innovative step.

As per reports, Optimus G2 will come with Android 4.2.2 out of the box, and it will run on a Snapdragon 800 chipset, which is integrated inside a processor with four Krait 400 cores and clocked at 2.3GHz. 2GB RAM or possibly 3GB RAM may be other key factor besides a solid GPU.

The new LG handset will also be one of the first handsets with built-in 4G LTE-Advanced connectivity. Samsung has just rolled out an LTE Advanced variant of its flagship the Galaxy S4. The battery inside the new LG phone would be a removable 2,700mAh unit that will feed all its aspects well.

The rear camera of the Optimus G2 is pegged at 13MP along with some unusual buttons – a volume rocker-like button on rear panel – likely for high-end camera features. It may have multiple functions, but nothing is known of now. On the bottom, there are two speakers on the handset.

It is also anticipated that the Optimus G2 will grow as the next Google Nexus 5 phone in future. Following the Optimus G’s adaptation to Nexus 4 last year, it can be a good guess, indeed. But, it is not still sure whether LG would really build the next edition Nexus phone for Google this time.
