Sibal Shifts Onus Of Curbing Objectionable Content On Media Itself

0 Flares 0 Flares ×

Perhaps the Government is trying to garner the views of the users as well as those who distribute content, for curbing the menace of objectionable content online.

While being asked about the Government’s action about adult applications on Android platform Telecom Minister Kapil Sibal reversed the onus of coming up with a workable solution, saying, “I want you people to suggest how we can close it. If we want to close it, you will attack us. I want all the media to come together and tell the minister how to deal with it so that if I do something about it, you don’t attack me”

In essence, he might be trying to entice the distributors of content itself to come up with a solution so as to avoid a public outcry if the Government takes a drastic step to curb the menace. In the past we have seen the government’s dictatorial nature when it tried to curb piracy. Instead of going to the core of the problem, it has been found, on numerous occasions, to prohibit access to entire websites for a few incriminating links.

Why is Google’s Android in the midst of this? Incidentally, the Government was being questioned about the rising instance of Google’s flagship & popular mobile Operating System (OS) being used as a medium via which pornography was rearing its ugly head. While the OS itself is not at fault, it’s the rising number of apps in the Google’s App Repository known as Google Play.

Allegedly, the App platform hosts a lot of applications which have suggestive & obscene content in addition to links that redirect the user to porn websites. Meanwhile Google has clarified that it takes a firm stand against such applications. Their formulated response was:

“Google Play developer program policy does not allow content that contains nudity, graphic sex acts, or sexually explicit material. Google has a zero-tolerance policy against child pornography. If Google becomes aware of content with child pornography, it will report it to the appropriate authorities and delete the Google accounts of those involved with the distribution”

It might be interesting to note that Google’s statement continually referred to a specific category & not the section in its entirety. This might be because, unlike India, such apps, up to a certain level are tolerated as long as the content consumer is above legally permissible age. For this precise reason, Google Play has a content rating mechanism with four levels, highest of which will certainly be against the norms here.
