Samsung Galaxy S3 How To Update To New Android 4.2.1 Update


With Android 4.1 Jelly Bean only recently completing its cycle across the US among Samsung Galaxy S3 users, the next big question is when will S3 users see Android 4.2.

US Samsung Galaxy S3 Android 4.2.1 courtesy of CM10.1

Android 4.2 rolled out recently and despite sounding like a minor update it has some notable features. This includes gesture typing, multi-user support, quick settings option, lock-screen widgets, PhotosSphere, Daydream and much more.

As you would guess, it could be anywhere between weeks and months before the Samsung Galaxy S3 sees Android 4.2, especially if you are with Verizon (sorry guys!). The good news is that if you don’t mind being adventurous, CyanogenMod now supports the Samsung Galaxy S3 as part of their nightly builds for CM10.1. This is basically Android 4.2.1 with CM features on top.

At this time the Samsung Galaxy S3 CM10.1 Android 4.2.1 Jelly Bean build supports Verizon, Sprint, T-Mobile, AT&T and US Cellular users. So if you’re interested, hit the source link for a guide to install it and give it a go. As always make sure to back up your data before doing so.
