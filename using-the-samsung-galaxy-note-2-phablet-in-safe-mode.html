Using the Samsung Galaxy Note 2 Phablet in Safe Mode

From time to time our powerful smartphones remind us that they are computer-driven. Just as your laptop or desktop PC from time to time can do weird things which require troubleshooting, smartphones like the powerful Galaxy Note II phablet may exhibit strange behaviors and performance anomalies that frustrate you, as well as limit the ability of the handset. Fortunately, the Galaxy Note II comes equipped with a Safe Mode reboot and system checkup which can help you identify and remove whatever may be causing the errors you are experiencing.

The Samsung Galaxy Note II is available for a reduced sale price now starting at $79.99.

If you own a Windows based computer, you may be familiar with the safe mode which starts your PC with only those programs required to power up and boot it up successfully. The Samsung Galaxy Note II Safe Mode works in basically the same manner, only loading those stock applications and base level programs that the phone needs to power up. The next time that your Galaxy Note II 4G does not work properly, or does something strange, follow the steps below to power up your handset for basic operation.

Entering the Galaxy Note II Safe Mode means enabling it, and to do this you must first turn off your handset by pressing the power button, and choosing Power Off from the list of choices that appear. After the Galaxy Note II smartphone has shut down, you want to hit the Power button once more. However, this time press and hold your finger down on the Power button while also simultaneously doing the same with the Volume Down button.

Eventually you will see the Samsung Galaxy Note II logo appear on your touchscreen display. At this time you can remove your finger from the Power button, but do not let go of the Volume Down button just yet. Watch your screen, and you will be rewarded with a status message that tells you your Galaxy Note II is operating in Safe Mode. Do not freak if you you do not see third-party applications, games and other features you are used to, as the phone is now operating in a stripped-down version of its former self.

When you see the Safe Mode notification, you can remove your finger from the Volume Down key, and then tap your OK button. Depending on the version of your operating system, the Android experience you will receive while in Safe Mode on your Galaxy Note II smartphone will vary. Galaxy Note II handsets with Android 4.1 or later will present you with a prompt asking you if you want to reboot under Safe Mode or not. If you need to disable your Galaxy Note II Safe Mode, just turn off your phone normally, then power it on in the normal fashion.

The Samsung Galaxy Note II can be purchased at a discounted price tag now starting at $79.99.
