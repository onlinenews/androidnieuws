Nexus 7 2 Rumored to Get Released in Late July

Nexus 7 2 Rumored to Get Released in Late July

By Brian Fulcher | 07/15/2013


A report from Taiwanese news publication Economic Daily suggests that the next-generation Google Nexus 7 tablet, unofficially known as the New Nexus or Nexus 7 2, may be out in the market by late July. No specific release date was mentioned, but it might be a good guess that the device will arrive in stores in the last week of the month.

The report also claimed that the Nexus 7 2 will be priced substantially higher than the original Nexus 7 tablet; while the base Nexus 7 cost just $199, the Nexus 7 2 will reportedly cost $299 and up, a hefty increase of $100 over the original tablet’s introductory price. This would put the next-generation Nexus 7 at a more similar price point to that of the basic Apple iPad mini, which costs $330 for a tablet with 16 GB and Wi-Fi connectivity only.

However, it also bears mentioning that the possible $100 price hike is much higher than what was previously rumored, which was only $20 more expensive than the first-generation Nexus 7.


Such a price increase may be justified by some of the rumored specifications that had been circulating since May and June. According to these rumors, the Nexus 7.2 will come with a 7-inch, 1920 x 1200 display, a quad-core Snapdragon 600 processor, 2 GB RAM, 4G LTE support on some versions, 32 GB internal memory and a 3,950 mAh. In addition, the Nexus 7.2 is expected to ship with Android 4.3 Jelly Bean out of the box.
