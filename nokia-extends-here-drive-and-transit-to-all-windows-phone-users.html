Nokia extends HERE Drive(+) and Transit to all Windows phone users

(*Google has a comparison of web maps here). 

Here Drive+ comes free with Nokia’s Lumia premium Windows phones (62x, 82x, 92x) - that is almost reason enough to buy one, and will available as a Windows Phone 7.x and 8.x Store app for other Windows handsets. It also has versions for iOS (an HTML5 based web app), Android and Firefox OS.

Drive+ uses true offline maps provided by NAVTEQ, acquired by Nokia in 2007, and rebranded as Here in 2012. Here provides solutions for GIS and government clients and powers major mapping providers such as Esri, Bing, MapQuest and Yahoo! Maps. Here licenses its technology to business including Garmin, BMW, Oracle, Flickr, SAP and Amazon.com.

Drive+ has introduced a new feature called My Commute that learns a driver’s daily route and habits and provides predictable and personalised commute recommendations and times based on current traffic conditions. Recognising that people travel overseas, the licence can be easily upgraded to cover 95 countries in 52 languages.

Under Windows Phone 8.x Here uses live tiles to save destinations and notifications to the start screen and caches maps for offline (no data cost) use. It marries well with Nokia’s City Lens (LiveSight) that lets users hold up their phone to reveal information about the buildings including contact information, hours and reviews in their line of sight from the phone camera.

Here Transit has public transportation information for more than 700 cities in 50 countries. It combines bus, train, ferry, tram and walking information in one app.

Here also captures location content such as road networks, buildings, parks and traffic patterns and has recently started capturing indoor maps of major buildings. Like Googles Waze app, Here draws on user experiences to make navigation more accurate.

Prices are not confirmed for Australia but Drive is likely to cost around $50 for a global licence.
