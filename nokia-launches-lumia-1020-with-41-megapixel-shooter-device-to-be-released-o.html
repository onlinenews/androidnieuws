Nokia Launches Lumia 1020 with 41-Megapixel Shooter, Device to Be Released on July 26

Nokia announced its newest flagship phone on Thursday, the Lumia 1020, a device that had been talked about for several months because of its rumored 41-megapixel rear camera. Previously known as the Nokia EOS, the Lumia 1020 proved to be more than just the stuff of rumors, launching at New York with the aforementioned camera and a variety of camera-related features that may, at the end of the day, be more important than the 41-megapixel sensor.

According to Nokia CEO Stephen Elop, who helped launch the device, the Lumia 1020 will also allow device owners to snap photos with improved clarity, even in low-light settings. “You will see things you’ve never seen before,” hinted Elop at the launch event. “You will get the smartest, sharpest smartphone pictures you’ve ever seen before.”


The Lumia 1020 also features a 4.5-inch, 720p display, a dual-core Snapdragon S4 Plus processor clocked at 1.5 GHz, 2 GB RAM, Windows Phone 8 and 32 GB internal memory. The phone will be released on July 26 as an AT&T exclusive, though buyers may want to set aside a substantial amount of money for the device – two-year contract pricing is a rather premium $299.99 upfront.

While Windows Phone 8 has not performed as well as Google’s Android and Apple’s iOS in terms of operating system market share, analysts are confident in the chances of the Lumia 1020 being a successful device for Nokia, at least in a relative sense.

According to Moor Insights and Strategy analyst Patrick Moorhead, the Lumia 1020 “is a good move” for Nokia, as consumers are now more attuned to how well their smartphones shoot photos with the rear camera. Moorhead, however, added that a high-end camera and all its associated features “cannot make up for Windows Phone 8’s lack of applications.”
