Google Nexus 5 Could Be Announced in October with Key Lime Pie out of the Box

Many would say it’s too early to talk about the Google Nexus 5 in depth, but more rumors have cropped up last week suggesting that the Nexus 5 will again be manufactured by LG, and launched in October with stock Android 5.0 Key Lime Pie out of the box.

Photo credit: Suman Chatterjee / Concept Phones

According to South Korean publication Daum, LG is working on the Nexus 5 together with its own flagship, the Optimus G2, a device scheduled to be launched in the United States next month. While the Optimus G2 will be announced in Korea this month and internationally in August, the Nexus 5 may come a few months thereafter – October, to be precise, to coincide with the month in which Google first confirmed the Nexus 4.


Also, Daum claims the Nexus 5 will ship with Android 5.0 Key Lime Pie, just as other publications have reported in the past. Google is expected to roll out an incremental Jelly Bean upgrade, Android 4.3, in the coming weeks, and wait till Android’s fifth anniversary in October before releasing Android 5.0.

For those hoping for some rumored specifications, Daum had a lot to share in that regard. The site claims that Google’s next-generation flagship will base its specs on the Optimus G2, which suggests a quad-core Snapdragon 800 processor, a 5- to 5.2-inch display with Full HD resolution, 2 GB or 3 GB RAM and a 13-megapixel rear camera. That said, it’s possible Google would have LG manufacture the Nexus 5 with a separate design, and some features of the Optimus G2, presumably microSD support, may not be available on the Nexus 5.
