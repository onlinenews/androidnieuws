Samsung Galaxy Note 3 Release Rumors Phablet May Be Offered by ‘Big Four’ US Carriers

Samsung Galaxy Note 3 Release Rumors: Phablet May Be Offered by ‘Big Four’ US Carriers

By Brian Fulcher | 07/15/2013


Some Galaxy Note 3 release and availability details were leaked last week by Samsung-centric tech blog Samsung Updates, along with model numbers corresponding to the respective Galaxy Note 3 carrier variants.

According to the report, there will be at least eight model numbers for the Samsung Galaxy Note 3. The SM-N900 would be the international edition with Samsung Exynos eight-core processor, while the SM-N9005 will correspond with the international Galaxy Note 3 with a quad-core Snapdragon under the hood; based on separate rumors, this processor could be the Snapdragon 800.


As for the U.S. variants, SM-N900A, SM-N900T, SM-N900V and SM-N900S would correspond with the AT&T, T-Mobile, Verizon and Sprint editions respectively. Two other model numbers – SM-N900R4 and SM-N900P – remain unknown commodities, as per the report.

Samsung Updates also provided some details regarding the Galaxy Note 3’s specifications. The site echoed rumors of the Galaxy Note 3 coming with a 5.7-inch display and 1080p display resolution, though it added that the phone would ship with Android 4.2.2 Jelly Bean. Depending on the rumor source, the Galaxy Note 3 is expected to ship with either Android 4.2.2 or Android 4.3 Jelly Bean.

Prior to last week’s listing of purported model numbers and specifications, it had been widely rumored that the Galaxy Note 3 would include a 13-megapixel rear camera with Optical Image Stabilization and AMOLED technology for the screen, though a more recent piece of scuttlebutt hinted at separate variants based on display technology, build material and camera type .

The Samsung Galaxy Note 3 is projected to be launched at the first week of September, possibly at this year’s IFA conference in Berlin.
