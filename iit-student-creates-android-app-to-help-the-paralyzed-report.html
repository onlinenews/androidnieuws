IIT student creates Android app to help the paralyzed Report

A student from IIT Gandhinagar has developed an Android application to help paralysed people from the neck down to enable greater freedom of movement, ToI reports. Pritesh Sankhe, a final year electrical engineering student developed the app by combining technology from robotics and Android smartphones to develop a full fledged app to control wheel chairs for paralyzed people. The app is still in a prototype stage.

Sankhe started developing the application under the guidance of Professor Uttama Lahiri, with the premise that its users will not be able to move their limbs but are capable of limited movement of the head and neck. According to Sankhe, to make the app work a bright colored object is placed on the users forehead so that the front camera of the smartphone can track the users movements and respond to commands.

Using voice commands, the app enables better mobility for such patients. It will mobility around the house more simpler for such patients for instance moving from the drawing to the kitchen. Another benefit of this system is that it will adapt to the usage patterns of the patient.
