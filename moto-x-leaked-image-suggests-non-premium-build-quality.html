Moto X Leaked Image Suggests Non-Premium Build Quality

A new image for Motorola’s upcoming flagship phone, the Moto X, has just leaked, although this photo suggests the device will be even less of a flagship than what is already expected. The photo does show off a few basic features of the Moto X, but more importantly, points to the possibility of a polycarbonate chassis with a matte black finish for the pictured device.

Photo credit: Weibo

Here’s a quick backdrop of the current situation with the unreleased Moto X. Prior to its confirmation at the D11 conference in May, most rumors had suggested that the Moto X (then known as “X Phone”) would be a superphone of sorts, with some reports tipping ridiculously high-end specifications and features.


More recently, though, a more realistic, albeit midrange set of specs started making the rumor rounds, with the Moto X believed to include a dual-core Snapdragon S4 Pro processor, 2 GB RAM, 720p display resolution with an unknown screen size and Android 4.2.2 Jelly Bean. Still, many still see the Moto X as a flagship, with the device’s customization options and special features expected to make up for the midrange specs. But what’s to make of this new leak from Chinese Twitter equivalent Weibo?

Looking at the image, the Moto X’s headphone jack and rear camera can be seen, which doesn’t suggest any unusual feature placement. But it’s the phone’s matte black finish that’s worth checking out – the device in the photo looks to be made out of some sort of plastic or polycarbonate, though the matte finish is a nice touch.

Arguably, this device doesn’t look as cheap as competing devices with glossy plastic finishes. However, if this Moto X leak is legitimate, it adds to the possibility of the handset being even less of a flagship in the traditional sense than what is already expected.
