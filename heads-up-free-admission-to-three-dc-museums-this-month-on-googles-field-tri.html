Heads Up: Free Admission to Three D.C. Museums This Month on Google’s Field Trip App

Posted by Tim Regan / Thursday, July 11th, 2013

Photo courtesy of President Lincoln's Cottage, screenshot of Google's Field Trip

Museums are great. Free museums are better. This month, you can get free admission to three D.C. museums by using Google’s Field Trip app for iPhone and Android.

Here’s a breakdown of how it works: using location-based data, the program curates a list of nearby interesting and eclectic hotspots sourced from publications like Thrillist, Zagat and Eater. Locations can include restaurants, local hangouts, historic locations and of course, museums. To promote awareness of Field Trip, Google has partnered with some of the nation’s acclaimed museums and zoos for free admission through the end of July. Local establishments include President Lincoln’s Cottage, the National Building Museum and the National Museum of Crime and Punishment.

This offer only lasts until the end of the month, so check it out before it’s too late.
